Anxiety is a natural response to stress or perceived danger, designed to alert and protect us. 
However, when it becomes excessive, persistent, or uncontrollable, it may develop into an anxiety disorder. 
This mental health condition affects millions worldwide, interfering with daily life, relationships, and well-being.
Anxiety manifests in various forms, such as generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), social anxiety, panic disorder, and phobias.
Common symptoms include constant worry, restlessness, difficulty concentrating, rapid heartbeat, and physical discomfort like headaches 
or stomach issues. Triggers can range from work pressures and personal challenges to genetics and past trauma.
While anxiety can feel overwhelming, effective treatments exist. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) helps individuals identify
and manage negative thought patterns, while relaxation techniques like mindfulness and deep breathing promote calmness. 
Medications, such as anti-anxiety drugs or antidepressants, may also provide relief when prescribed by a healthcare professional.
Lifestyle changes, including regular exercise, a balanced diet, and sufficient sleep, can significantly reduce symptoms. 
Building a supportive network of friends, family, or support groups also fosters resilience.
